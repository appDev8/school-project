## Part 1 — Learning-space design

**NSW regulatory frame (FACT).**
- NSW school built works benchmark to the DoE **Educational Facilities Standards & Guidelines (EFSG)** — room sizes, Room Data Sheets, required adjacencies. EFSG organises secondary schools into **"hubs"** (e.g. HS101 General Learning Spaces; HS301 Science Learning Hub; HS304 Food & Textiles; PS203 Hall+Canteen) — each a cluster of GLS + breakout + support/store. (education.nsw.gov.au/about-us/efsg)
- **National Construction Code:** school teaching buildings are **Class 9b** (assembly); boarding would be Class 3. (ncc.abcb.gov.au) Non-government schools also register premises with **NESA**.
- Also engage **AS 1428** accessibility and acoustics to **AS/NZS 2107:2016** (basis of EFSG DG11; classroom reverberation ~0.5–0.6 s).

**Space-program logic (REC, indicative).** Innovative/agile secondary schools run **~9–12 m² gross/student** and cluster 100–120 students with 4–6 teachers around a shared **learning commons** (glazing/movable walls for supervision). Indicative net areas:
- **Learning studios / GLS:** ~2.0–2.5 m²/student (~50–65 m² for ~26), opening onto a **commons** (~80–120 m²) with sink, power, storage, soft + standing seating.
- **Makerspace / fabrication:** ~**5.6 m²/student** (~140–150 m² for 25); zone "clean" (electronics/design) vs "dirty/wet" (cutting, 3D print, dust/fume extraction). (k12maker.mit.edu)
- **Media/creative-arts studio:** ~80–110 m²; acoustic isolation, blackout, green-screen/recording alcove.
- **Science/innovation lab:** EFSG science hub = GLS (theory) + lab (benches, sinks, fume cupboard, safety shower) + prep + chemical/apparatus store, **direct lab-to-prep access required**; indicative lab ~85–95 m².
- **"Moot court"/law space:** ~70–90 m² flat-floor, reconfigurable bench/bar/jury — pairs with the justice precinct (Part 2).
- **Exhibition/presentation hall:** double-height ~250–400 m² for whole-cohort PBL exhibitions; doubles as assembly.
- **Advisory/home-base rooms:** ~30–45 m² for ~15-student advisories.
- **Staff collaboration:** open planning + focus rooms; ~**8–10 m²/FTE** incl. workroom/meeting.
- **Wellbeing/counselling:** 12–16 m², acoustically private, discreet second egress, near pastoral hub.
- **Prayer space (musalla) + wudu:** ~**0.8–1.0 m²/worshipper** (Jumu'ah peak governs); **wudu ~1 station per 40–60 users**, solid-surface basins, drainage separated from prayer floor; gendered access; qibla orientation; shoe storage. At 600 students plan musalla ~480–600 m² (or split by gender / overflow into hall).
- **Commercial kitchen + dining (whole-school meals):** kitchen **~0.5–0.7 m²/seat** (min ~20 m²); dining + circulation ~**1.0–1.4 m²/seat**; aisles ≥2 m; comply with NSW food-premises fit-out. For sittings of ~200: dining ~280–360 m² + kitchen ~120–150 m².

**Adjacency/zoning (REC).** Wet/noisy/messy (makerspace, kitchen, science) grouped with services; quiet (advisory, wellbeing, library) buffered; **musalla near a calm threshold with adjacent wudu**, water separated from prayer floor; hall/exhibition near public entry for after-hours community use; staff zones with sightlines to commons.

**Indicative floorplate, 2–3 storeys, 120→600 (REC).** Build to the **600 endstate** structurally; fit out in stages.
- *Ground (public/shared):* entry, admin, hall/exhibition, commercial kitchen + dining, musalla + wudu, makerspace (heavy services, yard access).
- *Level 1:* science/innovation labs, media/arts, moot court, library/commons.
- *Level 2:* learning studios + advisory home-bases + wellbeing + staff collaboration.
- Open Stage 1 (~120) on one upper level + shared ground floor; activate further studio clusters as cohorts grow. Stack wet services + a single goods/lift core to control cost.

## Part 2 — Parramatta city-as-campus (real assets)

- **Western Sydney University — Parramatta City (Parramatta Square):** 14-storey vertical campus, ~10,000 students (mainly business); plus **South (Rydalmere)**. *Supports:* enterprise/entrepreneurship, STEM mentoring, tertiary aspiration. (architectureau.com; westernsydney.edu.au)
- **Powerhouse Parramatta (opens late 2026):** ~30,000 m²; 7 exhibition halls; 900-seat theatre; cinema; **200-seat demonstration kitchen**; artist studios. *Supports:* design, science, applied tech, food/media. (theurbanlist.com; atparramatta.com)
- **Parramatta Justice Precinct:** Children's Court, Sydney West Trial Courts, DCJ, Legal Aid, DPP. *Supports:* law/moot-court, civics, history. *Access:* court visits need pre-booking/protocols. (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parramatta_Justice_Precinct)
- **Parramatta Park + Old Government House (UNESCO World Heritage):** National Trust runs curriculum-linked, object-based programs. *Supports:* history, First Nations, heritage, ecology. (greatersydneyparklands.nsw.gov.au; nationaltrust.org.au)
- **Riverside Theatres (redevelopment from late 2026):** future lyric/playhouse/black-box/cinema + rehearsal. *Supports:* performing arts, production tech. (cityofparramatta.nsw.gov.au)
- **City of Parramatta Council** — civics/local-government, precinct planning.
- **Westmead health & innovation precinct** (Westmead + Children's Hospitals, research). *Supports:* health-science, biomed. *Access:* one rail stop / light rail; clinical-area restrictions. (planning.nsw.gov.au)
- **Industry/professional employers** — **NAB** (3 Parramatta Square, ~6,000 staff) and **Westpac** anchor a finance/tech cluster (~17,000 finance jobs). *Supports:* work-integrated learning, data/AI, finance/enterprise mentoring. (atparramatta.com; westpac.com.au)

**How city-as-campus works in practice (NSW policy — FACT + REC).** Govern under the **NSW Excursions Policy**: documented **risk assessment + risk-management plan before approval**, signed parent consent, medical info. **Supervision** is set by the risk assessment, **not a single fixed ratio** (meeting a numeric ratio "may not be adequate"); the school's **duty of care cannot be delegated** to venues/volunteers; mandatory reporting applies throughout. *REC:* exploit CBD walkability for low-risk, high-frequency **micro-excursions**; standardise routes + embark/disembark headcounts; vet venue/partner staff against **Child Safe Standards / WWCC**; schedule recurring sites once per 12 months to streamline repeat risk assessments. (policies.education.nsw.gov.au; education.nsw.gov.au)

> Key caveat: confirm all indicative m² and adjacencies against current EFSG **Room Data Sheets** and a Class 9b BCA/fire-egress assessment before briefing an architect.
