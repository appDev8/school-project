# PART B — THE COMPLETE KNOWLEDGE BASE

## 1. Context & demography `[verify current figures]`
Sydney — especially Western and South-Western Sydney — has a large, young and growing Muslim community with strong, sustained demand for Islamic schooling. Most established Islamic schools are academically solid but **pedagogically traditional** (timetabled, exam-driven). There is a clear, unmet gap for a school that is unapologetically **faith-centred *and* genuinely innovative** in how it teaches — combining academic rigour, real-world capability and character. The role being filled is **Head of School (Founding Appointment)**; the brief calls for a visionary systems-thinker and practising Muslim to build experiential, project/inquiry-based, design-thinking, faith-informed learning on a NESA-accredited base, with educators as "learning coaches, guides, competency architects and experts-in-residence." *(Confirm specific population, demand and competitor figures against current ABS / NESA / sector data before publishing.)*

## 2. Vision & the connecting idea — faith at the centre
One principle holds everything together: **Itqan** — excellence and perfection in whatever your hands turn to, performed as worship. From that centre, three things schools usually separate become **one journey, not three subjects**:

| Faculty | Islamic frame | In our school |
|---|---|---|
| **Mind** | *‘Ilm* — beneficial knowledge | The wellspring (**Manhal**) |
| **Hands** | *‘Amal* — righteous action | Real projects in the real world (**’Amal Projects** + **Kitab Maftuh**) |
| **Heart** | *Iman & Akhlaq* — faith & character | Formed in the doing — evidenced in **Athar** |

The tradition supplies the logic: knowledge (*‘ilm*) is incomplete until it becomes action (*‘amal*); action is worthy only when done with excellence (*Itqan / Ihsan*); and the purpose of reading the world (*Iqra* — "read the signs") is to flourish and serve. This is *also* the project-based-learning arc — **learn → apply → reflect → contribute**. A child therefore practises faith-as-excellence in real contexts hundreds of times before graduating: the habit that produces leaders who are **capable *and* grounded**.

## 5. The pedagogy — four interlocking layers
1. **Manhal (the wellspring).** Every NESA outcome is a self-paced, mastery-gated **node**; students draw on demand.
2. **’Amal Projects (knowledge into action).** One real, interdisciplinary project a term, co-designed with mentors.
3. **Kitab Maftuh (the world as campus).** Studios, mosques, councils and workplaces as real learning sites.
4. **Athar (the evidence).** Application, reflection and contribution — the inspector's cleanest audit trail.

**Modality mix.** The week is blocks, not 40-minute periods: **knowledge sprints** (F2F, Guide-led explicit teaching), **’Amal Project studios** (collaborative), **self-paced Manhal time** (independent, coach circulating), **Usrah** (daily home-family with the Murabbi), **Kitab Maftuh** (off-site). On-demand tutorials sit on top. Frameworks underneath: Gold-Standard PBL, Stanford design thinking, Bloom's & SOLO taxonomies, Universal Design for Learning.

## 6. Learning requirements & NESA compliance `[NESA]`
- Register **before** opening (12-month provisional licence on "capacity to comply"); **applications due 31 March** of the year before opening; first-year inspection ~Term 2 (Strand A welfare, Strand B teaching).
- Mandatory hours: **400 each** of English, Mathematics, Science across Years 7–10; **100** of a language; plus PDHPE and the rest.
- **All teaching by NESA-accredited teachers.** RoSA (Stages 4–5) and HSC (Stage 6) per the syllabus.
- NESA sets **no class-size or staff ratio** — only accredited teachers covering the mandatory curriculum + safe supervision. *That is the legal space the model's flexibility lives in.*

## 8. The Manhal in practice (node anatomy)
Each **node**: a learning intention; the NESA outcome code(s) it delivers; a visible **Bloom** badge (Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyse → Evaluate → Create); prerequisites (the upstream links); multi-modal on-demand resources; a **mastery check** that gates it; and the projects it feeds. Students advance on demonstrated mastery, not the clock. Because every mandatory outcome is tagged to a node **and** a portfolio evidence point, an automated **coverage audit** guarantees no gaps versus a traditional curriculum — with a stronger audit trail.

## 17. Foundation roadmap & risk
**Phases & gateways:** 0 Feasibility → funded mandate + viable site · 1 Design & evidence → application ready · 2 Build & approve (submit 31 Mar; recruit; fit-out; enrolment) → provisional registration · 3 Launch & prove → pass first-year inspection.
**Three binding constraints:** the immovable **31 March** application date (~9–10 months before a January open); premises/planning (a State Significant Development can take **~24 months**); the opening-stage **Manhal**, which must be coverage-complete before opening.
**Top risks → mitigations:** NESA delay/refusal (engage early; single-stage launch defers accreditation) · no/late site (start Phase 0; interim lease) · Manhal not coverage-complete (coverage audit; single-stage scope) · can't recruit aligned accredited staff (early recruitment; grow-your-own; hire for character then train) · financial viability (conservative enrolment; staged scaling) · model-fidelity drift (fidelity rubric; PD; measure it) · unsafe/insufficient internships (vetted host network).
**The de-risking move — single-stage launch:** open **Year 7 only**, add one year-level a year. Shrinks the Year-1 Manhal to one stage, needs a smaller team and less capital, and **defers HSC accreditation ~4–5 years** so registration is secured first and accreditation is added as the cohort reaches Tafaqquh and Riyadah. *"Open one year group well — not a whole school."*
